# PrepSignals — Social Media Strategy & Content Calendar
**Date:** March 7, 2026
**Focus:** Twitter/X + LinkedIn positioning for evidence-based narrative ownership

---

## Brand Voice & Messaging Pillars

### Tone
- **Confident:** We know what works (backed by data, not guesses)
- **Data-driven:** Every claim tied to evidence or research
- **Accessible:** No LLM jargon; talk outcomes, not mechanics
- **Action-oriented:** Each post moves toward one of three behaviors (try, learn, share)

### Messaging Pillars

1. **Evidence > Vibes**
   - *Core idea:* Interview feedback should be backed by proof, not gut feel
   - *Why it matters:* Most tools give vague tips; we show you exact quotes where you stumbled
   - *Where it lands:* Product differentiator posts, competitive objection posts

2. **Outcomes > Features**
   - *Core idea:* The only metric that matters is "did you get hired?"
   - *Why it matters:* Mock interview scores are useless if they don't predict real results
   - *Where it lands:* Thought leadership, case studies, research posts

3. **Transparency > Black Box**
   - *Core idea:* You should understand how you're being scored and why
   - *Why it matters:* Other tools hide their rubric; we show you ours
   - *Where it lands:* Educational posts, rubric explainers, "how we score" content

4. **Practice > Perfection**
   - *Core idea:* Better to fail 10 times in practice than once in the real interview
   - *Why it matters:* Habit formation requires iteration and visible progress
   - *Where it lands:* Community celebration posts, progress tracking posts, user wins

## Platform Strategy

### **LinkedIn** (Primary: Thought Leadership + B2B Signal)
- **Audience:** PM, design, data candidates; recruiters; career coaches
- **Cadence:** 2-3x/week
- **Content mix:** 40% educational, 30% thought leadership, 20% product news, 10% user wins
- **Post format:** 3-5 paragraphs, line breaks for readability, 1 relevant emoji max
- **Goal:** Own "evidence-based interview prep" narrative; become cited source for interview coaching

### **Twitter/X** (Secondary: Real-Time + Community)
- **Audience:** Earlier-stage candidates; startup community; tech culture commentary
- **Cadence:** Daily (1-3 tweets)
- **Content mix:** 40% quick takes, 30% research/findings, 20% user wins, 10% engagement/fun
- **Post format:** Punchy, 1-2 tweets max, hooks that encourage RTs and replies
- **Goal:** Build engaged community; seed viral moments around "interview culture"

### **Week 3-4: "Transparent Rubric" Deep Dive**

*Educational content. Build trust through transparency.*

**LinkedIn Post (Mid-Mar) — "Here's our exact rubric"**

We built PrepSignals to answer one question: Why do some candidates get offers and others don't?

We reverse-engineered the answer from 500+ interviews.

Here are the 8 signals we measure:

1. **Evidence Extraction** — Do you back up claims with examples? ("I grew sales 30%, by partnering with enterprise accounts.")
2. **Active Listening** — Do you ask clarifying Qs or assume? (Good: "Wait, are we talking B2B or B2C?" Bad: Answers without clarifying.)
3. **Decision Velocity** — Do you decide confidently or endlessly consider options?
4. **Communication Efficiency** — Can you say it in 30 seconds? (Not 5 minutes.)
5. **Signal Integration** — Do you learn from feedback and apply it immediately?
6. **Handling Uncertainty** — When you don't know, do you say so or bluff?
7. **Culture Fit** — Do you ask about values, team structure, and mission fit?
8. **Role-Specific Depth** — For a PM: Do you ask about metrics, not just features?

Here's the thing: **Different seniority levels weight these differently.**

A Junior PM should emphasize Structure & Evidence. A Senior PM should emphasize Culture Fit & Decision Velocity.

We adjust the rubric by role. Most tools don't.

## Tools & Workflows

### **Content Calendar**
- Use [Notion/Linear/Asana] to schedule posts
- Batching: Write Mon/Wed for Tue/Thu/Fri posts (reduces activation energy)
- Templating: Create post templates for each pillar (save time)

### **Posting Tools**
- **Twitter:** Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite (batch schedule)
- **LinkedIn:** Native scheduler (posts have better reach)

### **Community Management**
- Daily: Check mentions, reply to comments (within 2 hours)
- Weekly: Retweet user wins, share community highlights
- Monthly: Synthesize feedback into product/content insights

### **Measurement**
- Set up Sprout Social or Hootsuite for tracking
- Weekly: Review engagement, note what resonates
- Monthly: Report on KPIs, adjust strategy
